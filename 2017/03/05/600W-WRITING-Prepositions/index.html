At/On/In

기본 규칙

  1. At - 하루 중 언젠가
    • at five o’clock
    • at lunchtime
    • at sunset
  2. On - 일/요일
    • on Friday
    • on 16 May 1999
    • on my birthday
  3. In - 달/년/계절
    • in October
    • in 1998
    • in winter
    • in the Middle Ages
    • in the past
    • in the future

다음의 경우에도 at을 사용함.

  1. at night
  2. at the weekend / at weekends
  3. at Christmas
  4. at the moment / at present
  5. at the same time

in the morning, 그러나 On Friday morning.

  1. in the morning
    • on Friday morning
  2. in the afternoon
    • on Sunday afternoon
  3. in the evening
    • on Monday evening

last, next, this, every 앞에는 at/on/in을 안쓴다.

  1. I’ll see you next Friday.
  2. They got married last March.

얼마 후에, in

  1. The train will be leaving in a few minutes. (몇분 후에)
  2. She’ll be here in a moment. (곧, 바로)

위치 표현

  1. Above / Over ~ 위에
  2. Across ~을 가로질러
    • Draw a line across the page.
  3. Against ~에 기대어
  4. Ahead of/In front of ~앞에

  6. Around ~ 둘레에

    • He sat beneath the tree.

  1. I want to see a movie tonight. Who is (with) me?
  2. I’m sorry. This cake isn’t (for) you. It is not your birthday.
  3. I got selected to play for the city soccer team. I’m (in)!
  4. That book is (by) Dickens.
  5. Are the lights (on)?
  6. No. They are (off).
  7. I’m (for) equal rights for men and women. We all have the same rights.
  8. I’m (against) child labor. I don’t think children should work.
    • for : support
    • against : oppose
  9. Mark is (on) the bus, but Kate is (in) a taxi.
  10. Is Pete here today? Yes, but I’m not sure where. He is (around). I saw him 5 minutes ago.
  11. You look sick. Are you ok? No. I’m a little (off) today.
    • be off : don’t feel good.
  12. I am so tired. I was (up) until 2am last night.
    • be up : stay awake.
  13. Don’t lie to me. I am (onto) you. I know what you did yesterday.
    • be onto somebody: ~의 잘못을 알고 있다.
  14. Susan is so (into) you. She is always looking at you and smiling.
    • be into something: ~에 관심이 많다.

Quiz (첫 테스트. 많이 틀림)

  1. I usually get up (at) 6:00
  2. Do you believe (in) ghosts?
  3. Germany is (by) France. (near, beside)
  4. Children rely (on) their parents for food and shelter.
  5. I usually take my vacation (in) July.
  6. He threw a ball (at) the window.
    • throw at - 맞으라고 던지다.
    • throw to - 잡을 수 있게 던지다.
  7. He as worked here (for) five years.
  8. Chris excels (at) sports.
  9. John is very fond (of) children.
  10. He is very proud (of) his high grades.
  11. My sister was born (on) May 21st.
    • in a month
    • on a day
  12. Andrea contributed many ideas (to) the discussion.
  13. There is a painting hanging (above) my desk.
  14. Please write your name (on) the dotted line.
  15. Did you forgive him (for) lying to you?
    • forgive someone for something : ~이 ~한것을 용서하다.

Nouns with Prepositions (매우 어려움)

  1. She gave me a check (for) a lot of money.
  2. The reasons (for) this meeting is to discuss the merger.
